Vegan Sushi

If you're like me, I was never the biggest fan of seafood. Let me be clear, it was the texture, not the taste.

Japanese cuisine, given it's geographical location is full of fish, but luckily there is a way to still enjoy the art of rolling nori while being plant-based.

Below is how I get my fill on little taste of Japan (serves 2)

  • pack of Nori
  • sushi rice (50g)
  • teriyaki Tofu (1/2 pack)
  • cucumber (1/4)
  • medium orange pepper (1)
  • medium avocado (1)
  • bamboo sushi roller
  • soy sauce
  • vegetable oil (25 mL)
  • salt to taste

Lets get rolling!

  1. Cook the sushi rice on a medium to high heat for 10 minutes, it should have a sticky consistency once the water has evaporated.
  2. Preheat all the vegetable oil in a pan on medium heat.
  3. Slice your tofu into 10 equal parts and place into the oiled pan when hot
  4. Chop your pepper and cucumber so they are matchstick size and length
  5. In a bowl, mash the avocado and add salt to your liking.
  6. Take tofu off of the heat once they receive a brown side to them.
  7. On your bamboo sushi roller, place a sheet of nori shiny side down.
  8. When the sushi rice cools, spoon onto the nori leaving 1/4 inch left on all sides.
  9. Spoon some of the mashed avocado ontop of the rice.
  10. Add in 5 slices of the tofu onto the roll.
  11. Add in the matchstick cucumber and peppers.
  12. Once all your ingredients are in, gently wet the opposite edge of the nori.
  13. Using your bamboo mat, gently roll and squeeze slightly as you go.
  14. Leave in the fridge for 5 minutes to set
  15. Slice and serve with soy sauce!
